Research

Stretching the Net: Multidimensional Regularization", second resubmission at Econometric Theory

In the paper I derive asymptotic risk (expected loss) results for shrinkage estimators with multidimensional regularization in high-dimensional settings.

I introduce the MuSE (multidimensional shrinkage estimator) class, which includes the elastic net, extending the analysis of Abadie and Kasy 2018.

I show that as the number of parameters to estimate grows the empirical loss converges to the oracle-optimal risk.

To help guide applied researchers in their choice of estimator I compare the empirical Bayes risk of the lasso, ridge and elastic net in a spike and normal setting. See the figure for the lasso vs. elastic net comparison for different sparsity settings.
